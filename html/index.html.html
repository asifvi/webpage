China Sector Profiles

Moody's sector profiles is a series of publications designed to providing comprehensive, in-depth research and insights into the key demand drivers and ESG factors of corporate and public infrastructure sector in China

Learn More

Airports

China civil aviation transportation has grown rapidly and ensures stability of national industrial and supply chains. China is aiming to achieve an integrated transportation and logistics system with breakthrough in smart technology and green infrastructure to improve capacity, service quality and effeciency of transportation sector by 2025.

Learn More

Coal

We expect China's coal production in absolute volume will continue to grow in the next 3-5 years, but at much slower pace, as the country accelerate investments in renewable energy. As China's energy demand are increasingly met by its renewable energy sources, China's total coal production will peak and fall before 2030, consistent with its carbon peak goal.

Learn More

Cement

Lower demand for cement products will make overcapacity more prominent and sustained. It will take time for the cement industry to gradually adapt to the structural decline in demand. Capacity reduction, either because of regualtory or economic reasons, is neded before the industry can rebalance itself against a lower level of demand.

Learn More

Steel

The Chinese government aims to increase the market share of top 10 steelmakers to 60% by 2025 from the current level of around 40%. To achieve carbon peaking and carbon neutrality before 2030 and 2060, respectively, steel companies in China applying low carbon technology and developing new products whioch can reduce steel usage and end users' carbon footprint.

Learn More

Automobiles

China is the lagest auto market with units sales reaching 26.9 million in 2022. Regulations that encourage automakers to produce NEVs, rising auto penetrations and sales, investment in technology R&D and product development, improved NEV price-to-performance, and a greater number of charging piles will support NEV sales.

Learn More

Express Delivery

The express delivery market in China is fragmented but market consolidation in the past few years has resulted in the formation of a few large players. China's express delivery sector will transit from a labour-intensive industry to a capital-and-technology intensive one.

Learn More

Oilfield services

The Chinese government's increased focus on energy security and thereby reducing its import dependency on oil and gas, will result in domestic oil and gas production, thus increasing the need for oilfield services. High oil prices and supply constraints have incentivized E&P companies to increase investment to boost production.

Learn More

Home appliances

Higher disposable incomes, urbanizations rates and housing completions are the key demand drivers of home appliances in China. The industry maintained steady growth even in difficult operating conditions over the last couple of years, supported by the product innovations and consumptions upgrades.

Learn More

Protein

While the protein industry is highly fragmented, established companies are further solidfying their presence in the market. New entrants are focussing on niche offerings of alternative protein products with new technologies and ingredients.

Learn More

Water utilities

China's waster utilities has recorded rapid growth in investment and is being liberalized gradually. The governmrent is keen on attracting private and foreign capital to support the effecient management, operation and sustainable development of the country's water infrastructure.
